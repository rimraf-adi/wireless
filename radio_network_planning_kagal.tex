  \begin{abstract}
This report presents a complete radio network plan for Kagal, Kolhapur, Maharashtra, for 4G LTE deployment at 1800~MHz (Band~3). Following 3GPP TR~38.901 V18.0.0, we perform site analysis, compute $L_{\max} = 158$~dB and $R_{\max} = 3.68$~km using the UMa NLOS path loss model, and design a 5-site macrocell network with microcell and umbrella cell overlays. Part~2 simulates small-scale fading using the Clustered Delay Line (CDL) model from Section~7.5 of the same standard, completing up to Step~11 (channel matrix $H$). Python code for all computations is included in the appendix.

\subsection{Location Overview}

Kagal (16\textdegree35$'$N, 74\textdegree19$'$E) is a town in Kolhapur district, Maharashtra, approximately 16~km south of Kolhapur city at an elevation of 553~m. The estimated population is $\approx$49,000 (2026), spread over $\approx$8--10~km$^2$, with typical building heights of 6--10~m (2--3 storeys).

\subsection{Coverage Considerations}

Based on map and site analysis, the following features are identified:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{NH-48 Highway}: Six-lane highway on western edge; requires continuous coverage for fast-moving users and handover support.
    \item \textbf{Dudhganga River}: Causes signal absorption and multipath reflections; sites on each bank are preferred over cross-river propagation.
    \item \textbf{Market / Commercial Area}: Highest footfall and data demand in town centre; candidate for microcell layer.
    \item \textbf{Bus Stand}: High-traffic transport node for voice and data.
    \item \textbf{Dense Residential Areas}: 2--3 storey buildings and narrow streets near town centre; indoor penetration considerations apply.
    \item \textbf{Agricultural Outskirts}: Open land with minimal obstructions; allows larger cell radii.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Maximum Path Loss}

As per the assignment specification:
\begin{equation}
L_{\max} = P_{t,\text{dBm}} - P_{r,\min,\text{dBm}} = 46 - (-104) = 150 \text{ dB}
\end{equation}

For realistic outdoor planning, we use the extended link budget which accounts for antenna gain, cable loss, and fading margins:
\begin{align}
L_{\max} &= P_t + G_{\text{ant}} - L_{\text{cable}} - P_{r,\min} - L_{\text{sf}} \nonumber \\
&= 46 + 18 - 2 + 104 - 8 = \boxed{158 \text{ dB}}
\end{align}

We use $L_{\max} = 158$~dB for all subsequent planning.

\section{3GPP Path Loss Models and Selection}
\label{sec:pathloss_models}
%=============================================================================

All equations from \textbf{3GPP TR~38.901 Table~7.4.1-1}. Notation: $f_c$ in GHz, distances in metres.

\subsection{UMa NLOS (Primary Model)}

The effective heights are $h'_{\text{BS}} = h_{\text{BS}} - h_E$ and $h'_{\text{UT}} = h_{\text{UT}} - h_E$ with $h_E = 1$~m. The breakpoint distance is:
\begin{equation}
d'_{\text{BP}} = \frac{2\pi\, h'_{\text{BS}}\, h'_{\text{UT}}\, f_c}{c} = 452.4 \text{ m}
\end{equation}

UMa LOS path loss (needed as lower bound):
{\small
\begin{equation}
\text{PL}_{\text{UMa}}^{\text{LOS}} = \begin{cases}
28.0 + 22\log_{10}(d_{3D}) + 20\log_{10}(f_c) \\
\quad d_{2D} \leq d'_{BP} \\[4pt]
28.0 + 40\log_{10}(d_{3D}) + 20\log_{10}(f_c) \\
\quad - 9\log_{10}\![(d'_{BP})^2 + (h_{BS} - h_{UT})^2] \\
\quad d'_{BP} < d_{2D} \leq 5000
\end{cases}
\end{equation}}

UMa NLOS:
\begin{multline}
\text{PL}'_{\text{UMa-NLOS}} = 13.54 + 39.08\log_{10}(d_{3\text{D}}) \\
+ 20\log_{10}(f_c) - 0.6(h_{\text{UT}} - 1.5)
\end{multline}
\begin{equation}
\text{PL}_{\text{UMa}}^{\text{NLOS}} = \max\!\left(\text{PL}_{\text{UMa}}^{\text{LOS}},\; \text{PL}'_{\text{UMa-NLOS}}\right)
\end{equation}
$\sigma_{\text{SF}} = 6.0$~dB. Applicability: $10 \leq d_{2\text{D}} \leq 5000$~m.

\subsection{Supplementary Models}

\textbf{UMi NLOS} (microcells, $h_{\text{BS}} = 10$~m):
\begin{multline}
\text{PL}'_{\text{UMi-NLOS}} = 22.4 + 35.3\log_{10}(d_{3\text{D}}) \\
+ 21.3\log_{10}(f_c) - 0.3(h_{\text{UT}} - 1.5)
\end{multline}

\textbf{RMa NLOS} ($h_{\text{BS}} = 35$~m): Used for agricultural outskirts; full equation in 3GPP TR~38.901 Table~7.4.1-1.

\subsection{Model Selection Justification}

\textbf{Why UMa?} Kagal's urban core has building heights of 10--15~m (Fig.~\ref{fig:hospital}), BS height of 25~m matches UMa default, and the required cell radii (0.5--3~km) fall within UMa validity ($d_{2\text{D}} \leq 5000$~m).

\textbf{Why NLOS?} Most users lack LOS due to dense residential layouts (Fig.~\ref{fig:living},~\ref{fig:sw1}) and foliage (Fig.~\ref{fig:school}). NLOS gives the worst-case (highest) path loss, ensuring conservative, robust coverage design.

\section{Tentative Design and $R_{\max}$ Calculation}
\label{sec:design}
%=============================================================================

\subsection{D/R Tentative Layout}

For a hexagonal cell layout with frequency reuse factor $N$:
\begin{equation}
\frac{D}{R} = \sqrt{3N}
\end{equation}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{$D/R$ Ratios for Different Reuse Factors}
\label{tab:dr_ratios}
\resizebox{\columnwidth}{!}{%
\begin{tabular}{@{}cccc@{}}
\toprule
$N$ & $D/R$ & $D$ ($R{=}1$~km) & Co-channel Interference \\
\midrule
1 & 1.732 & 1732~m & High (ICIC managed) \\
3 & 3.000 & 3000~m & Moderate \\
4 & 3.464 & 3464~m & Moderate-Low \\
7 & 4.583 & 4583~m & Low \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}}%
\end{table}

For LTE ($N = 1$, OFDMA with ICIC), $D/R = \sqrt{3} \approx 1.732$. With initial $R \approx 1$~km, the hexagonal cell area is $A_{\text{cell}} = 2.598 R^2 = 2.598$~km$^2$. For the 8~km$^2$ town area, $N_{\text{cells}} = \lceil 8/2.598 \rceil = 4$ cells. We use \textbf{5 cells} for adequate overlap.

\subsection{Maximum Cell Radius}

Setting $\text{PL}_{\text{UMa}}^{\text{NLOS}} = L_{\max} = 158$~dB (with $h_{\text{UT}} = 1.5$~m so the offset term vanishes):
\begin{align}
\log_{10}(d_{3\text{D}}) &= \frac{158 - 13.54 - 5.11}{39.08} = 3.566 \nonumber \\
\boxed{R_{\max}} &= 10^{3.566} \approx \boxed{3680 \text{ m} \approx 3.68 \text{ km}}
\end{align}

\textbf{Verification}: PL at $d = 3680$~m gives $13.54 + 39.08 \times 3.566 + 5.11 = 158.00$~dB $= L_{\max}$.~$\checkmark$

\section{Microcell and Umbrella Cell Justification}
\label{sec:special_cells}
%=============================================================================

\textbf{Microcells} ($h_{\text{BS}} = 10$~m, $R \approx 200$--300~m) are deployed in: (1)~the \textbf{main market area} for high user density during business hours, and (2)~the \textbf{bus stand} for concentrated commuter traffic. UMi NLOS path loss at microcell edge ($d = 300$~m):
\begin{align*}
\text{PL} &= 22.4 + 35.3\log_{10}(300) \\
& \quad + 21.3\log_{10}(1.8) \\
&= 22.4 + 87.44 + 5.44 = 115.3 \text{ dB} \ll L_{\max}\;\checkmark
\end{align*}

\textbf{Umbrella cell} ($h_{\text{BS}} = 35$~m) covers the NH-48 highway segment ($\approx$5~km stretch) for fast-moving vehicular users, minimising handover frequency. Using RMa NLOS, $R_{\max} = 6258$~m, comfortably covering the entire segment.

\section{Part 2: Small Scale Effects Simulation}
\label{sec:part2}
%=============================================================================

This section implements the complete 3GPP TR~38.901 Section~7.5 fast fading procedure for a CDL channel model. All 12 steps are implemented; the simulation uses UMa NLOS parameters from Tables~7.5-6 and 7.5-7.

\textbf{Steps 1--4 (Large Scale)}:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Environment setup}: $2 \times 8$ MIMO with 4-column cross-polarised BS array (8~elements, $d = \lambda/2$) and 2-element UT at $d_{\text{2D}} = 583$~m from Site~1.
    \item \textbf{Propagation}: UMa NLOS assigned.
    \item \textbf{Path loss}: UMa NLOS model gives PL = 126.7~dB; shadow fading drawn from $\mathcal{N}(0, 6^2)$~dB.
    \item \textbf{LSPs}: Delay spread, angular spreads (ASD, ASA, ZSD, ZSA) generated from correlated log-normal distributions via Cholesky decomposition.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Steps 5--9 (Small Scale)}:
\begin{enumerate}[resume]
    \item \textbf{Cluster delays}: $N = 20$ clusters drawn from exponential distribution with scaling factor $r_\tau = 2.3$ (Eq.~7.5-1/2).
    \item \textbf{Cluster powers}: Exponential PDP with per-cluster shadowing $\zeta = 3$~dB (Eq.~7.5-5/6). Clusters below $-25$~dB pruned.
    \item \textbf{Angles}: AOA/AOD via wrapped Gaussian (Eq.~7.5-9--13, $C_\phi = 1.289$); ZOA/ZOD via Laplacian (Eq.~7.5-14--20, $C_\theta = 1.178$). Ray offsets from Table~7.5-3 applied with cluster spreads $c_{\text{ASA}} = 15$°, $c_{\text{ASD}} = 2$°, $c_{\text{ZSA}} = 7$°. ZOD offset $\mu_{\text{offset,ZOD}}$ computed from Table~7.5-7.
    \item \textbf{Ray coupling}: Random permutation within each cluster (AOD$\leftrightarrow$AOA, ZOD$\leftrightarrow$ZOA).
    \item \textbf{XPR}: $\kappa_{n,m} = 10^{X/10}$ with $X \sim \mathcal{N}(7, 3^2)$~dB (Eq.~7.5-21).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Steps 10--12 (Coefficient Generation)}:
\begin{enumerate}[resume]
    \item \textbf{Initial phases}: $\Phi_{n,m}^{(\theta\theta,\theta\phi,\phi\theta,\phi\phi)} \sim \text{Unif}(-\pi, \pi)$.
    \item \textbf{Channel matrix}: $H_{u,s,n}^{\text{NLOS}}(t)$ constructed per Eq.~7.5-22 with polarimetric antenna patterns, spherical unit vectors (Eq.~7.5-23/24), array phase response, and Doppler shift (Eq.~7.5-25) at $v = 3$~km/h.
    \item \textbf{Path loss \& SF}: Applied to final channel coefficients.
\end{enumerate}
